Fire in Australia From the Space

Visualisations of Data From VIIRS Sattelite

The bushfire in Australia is always a devastating problem in the country. It also gained many attention from the international after the 2019-2020 bushfire season. Bushfire causes massive losses, to forests, to lands, to wildlifes even human lives.

There are also many measures and precautions made by the government and the fire department to try to control the bushfire over the years. However, it is crucial for us to know how well those measures are performing and what are some important features government has to pay more attention on.

Fortunately, the NASA satellite data from MODIS and VIIRS makes us able to analyse the fire happened in Australia from the space. The satellite also provides near real-time data publically. Here we use the data from Aug 2019 to Sep 2019 to try to show how fire is affecting in Austalia bushfire season.

Fire in Austrlia 2019/08 - 2019/09

Let us first look at how the fire distributes over the country between August 2019 to September 2019. Each red dot in the left graph is a fire at that location. The darker the color of the red dot, the more brightness temperature of that fire. We can see fromt the graph that, there were more fires in August but the some fire in September had more brightness. Furthermore, most fire are observed near sea, and located at East and North region.

Trend of Number of Fires in Each State

One may ask, the bushfire exists in Australia for hundreds of years and why would we concern about the fires now. Here is a slope diagram showing the change in number of fires between 2010 and 2019 for each state in Australia. Each line represents the changes for each state. It is clear that despite the gradient of slope is different between states, the number of fires for all states has increased from 2010 to 2019. New South Wales has the most increase in numbers of fires among all states.

Number of Sighting of Koalas

Bushfire has impacted wildlifes badly. Fire destroys their habitats and cause death due to heat and smoke. For instance, the 2019-2020 bushfire season caused thousands of Koalas lost their lives. Here is a bar graph comparing the number of sightings of wild Koalas between 2019 and 2020. Even though the bushfire was not the only causing the decrease in number but was one of the main facters.
